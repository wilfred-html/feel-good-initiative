The Problem

The communities beside the ocean can't safely access it.

Thousands of residents in Imizamo Yethu and Hangberg live minutes from Hout Bay Beach — but lack safe, accessible places to swim. An ageing revetment blocks the shoreline. In summer, the beach gets dangerously overcrowded.

There's no ocean safety infrastructure, no marine education, and no plan to fix it. Until now.

Our Solution

An eco-friendly tidal pool that gives the ocean back to the people.

The R50M Hout Bay Tidal Pool will blend into the natural shoreline, replacing the old revetment with a living coastal ecosystem — safe, beautiful, and built for everyone.

Natural Shoreline Integration

Designed to blend seamlessly into the coast, replacing ageing infrastructure with a living tidal ecosystem that works with nature, not against it.

Ocean Safety Infrastructure

A safe, protected swimming environment especially for children and non-swimmers from communities that lack water safety resources.

Marine Education Hub

An outdoor classroom for schools and communities — teaching ocean safety, marine ecology, and environmental stewardship firsthand.
