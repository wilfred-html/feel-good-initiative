Hout Bay Tidal Pool

A Safe Ocean for Everyone

A world-class tidal pool is coming to Hout Bay Beach — creating a safe, eco-friendly swimming spot for locals from Imizamo Yethu, Hangberg, and beyond who have long lacked easy beach access.

Natural Shoreline Integration

Designed to blend seamlessly into the coast, replacing the old revetment with a living tidal ecosystem.

Environmental Assessment

Full environmental impact study underway with marine biologists to protect existing marine life.

Ocean Safety & Marine Education

A hub for teaching water safety and marine ecology to community members and school groups.

Who We Are

Where Nature Heals & Community Thrives

The Feel Good Initiative is a registered South African nonprofit dedicated to creating equitable access to nature, ocean safety, and environmental stewardship. Based in the Western Cape, we bridge the gap between underserved communities and the natural spaces they deserve.

Ocean Access

Creating safe, inclusive coastal infrastructure for communities historically excluded from beach access.

Ocean Safety

Teaching water safety and drowning prevention to protect lives in coastal communities.

Marine Conservation

Protecting marine ecosystems through science-led environmental assessments and habitat restoration.

Education

Marine education programs that inspire the next generation to understand and protect our oceans.

Our Focus Areas

Creating Spaces Where Everyone Belongs

From infrastructure to education — our initiatives are designed around the people who need them most.

Infrastructure

Coastal Access Projects

Building tidal pools and beach access points that serve communities like Imizamo Yethu and Hangberg who lack safe ocean access.

Safety

Ocean Safety Programs

Water safety training and drowning prevention — especially vital during summer when Hout Bay Beach sees heavy foot traffic.

Education

Marine Education Hub

The tidal pool will double as an outdoor classroom for marine ecology, connecting youth to the ocean through hands-on learning.
