01 — Brand Strategy
Why we exist.

The Feel Good Initiative began with one person picking up litter on Hout Bay Beach. Today, it's a multi-front movement for environmental protection and community upliftment.

Mission

Protect, restore, and beautify Hout Bay's beach and public spaces. Create safe, accessible environments. Bring the community together. Create jobs. Inspire local pride.

Vision

Scale from a local initiative to a national model for environmental care and community upliftment — proving that consistent daily action creates lasting change.

Unique Value

Other organisations run occasional cleanups. We show up every single day. Our Beach Ranger walks the shoreline daily. Our bins are always there. Our projects are visible, local, and ongoing.

Tagline

Do Good. Feel Good.

Core Values

🤝

Community First

Every decision starts with the people it affects. We build for the community, with the community.

🌿

Environmental Stewardship

Protect first. Build second. The environment is the foundation, not an afterthought.

📅

Consistency & Accountability

Not once a month. Every single day. We show up, and we prove it.

🏠

Local Pride

Built by Hout Bay, for Hout Bay. We restore pride in shared spaces.

📈

Growth & Opportunity

Jobs, skills, and infrastructure that last beyond the project.

Usage Rules

60-30-10 Rule

60% — Neutrals & backgrounds (Teal Wash, whites, light greys)
30% — Deep Forest & Ocean Teal (text, headers, cards)
10% — Sunset Orange (CTAs, highlights, urgency)

Accessibility

Deep Forest on white/wash backgrounds meets WCAG AA contrast. Ocean Teal on dark backgrounds meets AA for large text. Never place Coastal Light on white — insufficient contrast. Sunset Orange is for accents only, not body text.

✅ Do Say

  • "We show up. Every single day."
  • "Invest in positive change"
  • "Partner with us"
  • "Support our impact"
  • "Real impact. Real change."
  • "Built by the community, for the community"
  • "Join the movement"
  • "Action creates change"

❌ Never Say

  • "Aid" or "relief"
  • "Hand-me-downs"
  • "We aim to" (we DO, not aim)
  • "Small initiative"
  • "Budget-friendly"

  • Political or divisive content

05 — Photography & Imagery
Visual storytelling.

Every image should feel real, professional, and purposeful — showing action, community, and the natural beauty of Hout Bay.

Image Style

Real, Not Stock

Use actual photos from beach cleanups, community events, and the Hout Bay coastline. Never use generic stock imagery. Authenticity is everything.

Professional Quality

Photos should be well-lit, properly composed, and high resolution. Professional enough for a sponsor pitch deck but natural enough to feel genuine.

Drone & Landscape

Aerial shots of Hout Bay Beach, Chapman's Peak, and the coastline add scale and visual impact. Use for hero sections and section backgrounds.

Subject Matter

Action Shots

People cleaning, building, collecting. Never posed or staged — always mid-action. The Beach Ranger walking the shore. Volunteers filling bags.

Community & Joy

Smiling faces, group shots, hands working together. Show the human side — this isn't a corporate initiative, it's a community movement.

Before & After

Document transformation. Show the problem and the solution side by side. This is powerful for social media and sponsor reports.

Treatment Rules

Colour Grading

Warm, natural tones. Slight boost to greens and teals. Never over-saturated or heavily filtered. The ocean should look like the real ocean.

Overlays

When using images as backgrounds, apply a gradient overlay matching brand colours (teal wash or dark) at 85–95% opacity for text readability.

Sensitive Content

Never show poverty, suffering, or exploitation. Show pride, action, and progress. Children require parental consent for photography.

06 — Sponsor Co-Branding
Partners, not logos.

Sponsors are active partners in the mission. Their brand sits alongside ours with mutual respect — never above, never diminished.

Logo Placement Rules

Clear Space

The TFGI logo must have clear space equal to the height of the "F" in "Feel" on all sides. No other element should intrude into this space.

Minimum Size

Print: 30mm width minimum. Digital: 120px width minimum. Below these sizes, use the icon mark only.

Co-Branding Layout

On shared materials, TFGI logo appears on the left or top. Sponsor logo on the right or bottom. Separated by a thin vertical divider line in Ocean Teal.

Background Compatibility

On light backgrounds: use the full-colour logo. On dark backgrounds: use the white version. On photography: ensure sufficient contrast with an overlay if needed.

Sponsor Tier Visibility

Visionary (R5M+)

Full co-branding across all channels. Major naming rights. Logo on all materials, signage, and communications. Advisory seat.

Builder (R1M)

Permanent on-site plaque. Naming rights on pool features. Logo on website, events, and media. VIP launch access. Dedicated CSI case study.

Champion (R250K)

Featured in project media and press. On-site branding during construction. Annual CSI impact report for stakeholders.

Supporter (R50K)

Brand on website and materials. Social media features. Quarterly impact reports. Groundbreaking event invitation.

07 — Merchandise & Signage
Physical touchpoints.

Every t-shirt, bin, billboard, and banner is an opportunity to reinforce the brand and spread the movement.

Apparel

T-Shirts

White tee with Ocean Teal logo on left chest, tagline on upper back. Alt: Ocean Teal tee with white logo. Quality cotton only.

Volunteer Gear

Hi-vis vests with TFGI logo. Sponsor logos on back below TFGI. Sunset Orange for volunteer-specific items.

Caps

Ocean Teal or Deep Forest base, embroidered white logo only. No tagline on small items.

Signage & Print

Community Bins

Deep Forest bg, white logo + tagline. Sponsor logos at bottom, max 30% of face area. Weather-resistant vinyl.

Billboards

Drone photo with dark gradient overlay. Logo top-left, tagline centered, CTA bottom-right. Sponsor bar at bottom.

Event Banners

850×2000mm pull-ups. Teal Wash bg, Deep Forest text. QR code to website. Sponsors along bottom.

Production Notes

Print Colours

Always use CMYK values from the colour palette. For Pantone matching: Ocean Teal ≈ Pantone 7473 C. Sunset Orange ≈ Pantone 1665 C.

Materials

Prefer recycled or eco-friendly materials where possible — consistent with the environmental mission. Vinyl for outdoor signage. 300gsm for business cards.

Quality Control

All physical items must be approved by Sandy Daniel before production. Proof must show accurate colour reproduction. No exceptions.

Online presence.

The website is live. Social media is next. Every digital touchpoint should reinforce the same brand identity.

Website

Live URL

feel-good-initiative.netlify.app — the single source of truth for the initiative online. All social bios and print materials should link here.

Key Sections

Design System

The website uses the exact colour palette, typography, and spacing defined in this brand book. It serves as the living reference for all digital design.

Social Media

Active: Facebook

@TheFeelGoodInitiative — 144 followers. Primary channel for community updates, event announcements, and sponsor recognition.

Active: TikTok

Beach Ranger David's channel — 14K+ followers. Raw, authentic daily content showing real beach cleanup work. This is the brand's most powerful organic channel.

Recommended: Instagram

Launch when ready with curated content. Focus on: drone shots, before/after, community events, sponsor spotlights. Use the brand colour palette for story highlights.

Content Pillars

Daily Action

Beach Ranger updates. Daily cleanup photos. "We showed up today" posts. Consistency is the brand — show it.

Community Impact

Stories from residents. Volunteer spotlights. Event recaps. Before/after transformations. Make people feel part of it.

Vision & Progress

Tidal pool updates. Sponsor announcements. Milestone celebrations. Media coverage. Show momentum and credibility.
